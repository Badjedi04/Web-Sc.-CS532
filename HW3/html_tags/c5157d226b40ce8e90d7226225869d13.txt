<h1>Biden Resettles 10K Afghans in U.S. Communities Despite Vetting Issues</h1>
<aside class="bnn-social-share" data-fbc="47" id="social-share-article-top">

The Department of Defense, …" class="wp-post-image" height="480" src="https://media.breitbart.com/media/2021/10/GettyImages-1235610609-640x480.jpg" title="U.S. Army At Fort McCoy In Wisconsin Shelters Afghan Refugees" width="640"/><figcaption><cite>Barbara Davidson/Getty Images</cite></figcaption></figure><div class="header_byline"><address data-n=""><a href="/author/john-binder/" title="Posts by John Binder">John Binder</a></address><time datetime="2021-11-01T18:01:24Z">1 Nov 2021</time><span class="byC" data-dsqi="18558594" data-dsqu="/politics/2021/11/01/biden-resettles-10k-afghans-u-s-communities-despite-vetting-issues/#disqus_thread" data-fbc="47"></span></div> <div data-mp3l="2:43" data-mp3u="https://media.breitbart.com/media/2021/11/biden-resettles-10k-afghans-u-s-communities-despite-vetting-issues-18558594.mp3" id="PollyC"></div>

<div class="entry-content">
<p class="subheading">President Joe Biden’s administration has resettled about 10,000 Afghans across American communities in the last two months despite issues with vetting.</p><div class="H" id="gmx-rev-top"></div>
<p class="a8d-pre">Biden’s massive resettlement operation plans to <a href="https://www.breitbart.com/politics/2021/09/16/joe-biden-to-resettle-afghans-in-46-states-many-going-to-swing-states/">bring</a> at least 95,000 Afghans to the U.S. for resettlement across 46 states. Multinational corporations and former Presidents Bill Clinton, George W. Bush, and Barack Obama are <a href="https://www.breitbart.com/politics/2021/10/27/big-banks-big-pharma-big-tech-team-up-with-biden-to-resettle-afghans-in-u-s/">bankrolling</a> the operation.</p>
<figure class="a8d" id="D-ROS-B1"></figure>
<figure class="a8d" id="M-ROS-B1"></figure><figure class="H" id="gmxrevmore"></figure>
<p>The Afghans are initially flown into Philadelphia International Airport in Pennsylvania or Dulles International Airport in Virginia before temporarily living on various U.S. military bases while awaiting resettlement. Today, about 53,000 Afghan refugees remain temporarily living at U.S. bases in Wisconsin, Texas, New Mexico, Indiana, New Jersey, and Virginia.</p>
<div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_17720674" style="max-width: 650px"><a href="https://media.breitbart.com/media/2021/08/GettyImages-1336699489.jpg"><img alt="DULLES, VIRGINIA - AUGUST 27: Refugees arrive at Dulles International Airport after being evacuated from Kabul following the Taliban takeover of Afghanistan August 27, 2021 in Dulles, Virginia. Refugees continued to arrive in the United States one day after twin suicide bombings at the gates of the airport in Kabul killed 13 U.S. military service members and nearly 100 Afghans. “We will not forgive,” President Joe Biden warned ISIS, who claimed responsibility for the attacks. “We will not forget. We will hunt you down and make you pay.” (Photo by Chip Somodevilla/Getty Images)" class="size-full wp-image-17720674" height="480" src="https://media.breitbart.com/media/2021/08/GettyImages-1336699489.jpg" width="640"/></a><p class="wp-caption-text">Refugees arrive at Dulles International Airport after being evacuated from Kabul following the Taliban takeover of Afghanistan August 27, 2021, in Dulles, Virginia. (Photo by Chip Somodevilla/Getty Images)</p>
<figure class="a8d" id="D-ROS-B2"></figure>
<figure class="a8d" id="M-ROS-B2"></figure></div>
<p>In the first two months of the operation, refugee contractors — who are paid by Biden’s State Department to resettle refugees — have <a href="https://archive.md/1IGK6">resettled</a> about 10,000 Afghan refugees in American communities. About 3,000 Afghans, in addition, have fled U.S. bases on their own.</p>
<p>The resettlement of roughly 5,000 Afghans a month comes as lawmakers and federal officials have warned of issues with vetting procedures where some deemed “national security risks” are slipping through the cracks.</p>
<p>Last week, for instance, a federal official reportedly told lawmakers that ten refugees who <a href="https://www.breitbart.com/politics/2021/10/28/report-afghans-biden-national-security-risks/">arrived</a> in the U.S. as part of the operation were later revealed to be “national security risks” and thus were taken into federal custody where they remain.</p>
<p class="a8d-pre">Federal agents who worked on screening Afghans for entry to the U.S. now admit that they were not trained in identifying fraudulent visas, green cards, and passports. On a number of occasions, several Afghans reportedly traveled to the U.S. using fake passports.</p>
<figure class="a8d adSo" id="M-ROS-B3"></figure>
<p>Likewise, at a hearing last week, Sen. Josh Hawley (R-MO) revealed that an internal memo by an administration email at the time of the Afghanistan withdrawal showed that there were no <a href="https://www.breitbart.com/politics/2021/10/26/email-indicates-biden-told-officials-in-afghanistan-to-fill-out-planes-without-vetting/">vetting</a> procedures for boarding Afghans onto U.S.-bound flights.</p>
<div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_17996378" style="max-width: 650px"><a href="https://media.breitbart.com/media/2021/09/Afghanistan-evacuation-board-plane-U.S.-Air-Force-via-ap.jpg"><img alt="" class="size-full wp-image-17996378" height="480" src="https://media.breitbart.com/media/2021/09/Afghanistan-evacuation-board-plane-U.S.-Air-Force-via-ap.jpg" width="640"/></a><p class="wp-caption-text">In this Aug. 22, 2021, file photo provided by the U.S. Air Force, Afghan passengers board a U.S. Air Force C-17 Globemaster III during the Afghanistan evacuation at Hamid Karzai International Airport in Kabul, Afghanistan. (MSgt. Donald R. Allen/U.S. Air Force via AP, File)</p></div>
<p>“This email was shared [with] me by an American official present in Afghanistan during the evacuation who was shocked by [the Biden] administration’s failure to vet Afghans before they were evacuated,” Hawley <a href="https://twitter.com/HawleyMO/status/1453054643420995584?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw%7Ctwcamp%5Etweetembed%7Ctwterm%5E1453055313716862983%7Ctwgr%5E%7Ctwcon%5Es2_&amp;ref_url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.breitbart.com%2Fpolitics%2F2021%2F10%2F26%2Femail-indicates-biden-told-officials-in-afghanistan-to-fill-out-planes-without-vetting%2F">wrote</a> in a statement. “Email details orders from Joe Biden to fill up the planes – even without vetting.”</p>
