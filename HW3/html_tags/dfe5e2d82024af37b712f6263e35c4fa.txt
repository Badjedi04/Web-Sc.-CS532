<h1>Slovenian Prime Minister: Antifa Is ‘International Terrorist Organisation’</h1>
<aside class="bnn-social-share" data-fbc="300" id="social-share-article-top">

<div class="entry-content">
<p class="subheading">Janez Janša, prime minister of former first lady Melania Trump’s home country of Slovenia, has labelled the far-left extremist group Antifa an “international terrorist organisation” following violence in France over the weekend</p><div class="H" id="gmx-rev-top"></div>
<p class="a8d-pre">Prime Minister Janša made his comments on social media network Twitter, <a href="https://twitter.com/JJansaSDS/status/1454748497316655104?s=20">saying</a>: “Antifa is a global terrorist organization. Supported by the capital of financial speculators forging profits at the expense of the chaos caused by the operation.”</p>
<figure class="a8d" id="D-ROS-B1"></figure>
<figure class="a8d" id="M-ROS-B1"></figure><figure class="H" id="gmxrevmore"></figure>
<p>The Slovenian leader was reacting to a <a href="https://twitter.com/MrAndyNgo/status/1454506786686373889?s=20">post</a> by American journalist Andy Ngo which highlighted Antifa violence over the weekend in the French city of Nantes which saw the far-left extremists attempt to disrupt a meeting of the conservative writer and French presidential hopeful<a href="https://www.breitbart.com/tag/eric-zemmour/"> Eric Zemmour</a>.</p>
<p class="a8d-pre">According to a <a href="https://www.lefigaro.fr/politique/nantes-pres-de-200-personnes-protestent-contre-la-venue-d-eric-zemmour-au-zenith-20211030">report</a> from the newspaper <em>Le Figaro</em>, around 600 far-left extremists gathered in Nantes to oppose Zemmour on Saturday and some chanted for his death and clashed with police.</p>
<figure class="a8d" id="D-ROS-B2"></figure>
<figure class="a8d" id="M-ROS-B2"></figure>
<blockquote class="twitter-tweet" data-dnt="true" data-width="550">
<p dir="ltr" lang="fr">NANTES – Une voiture de police est prise pour cible par les manifestants anti <a href="https://twitter.com/hashtag/Zemmour?src=hash&amp;ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw">#Zemmour</a>. Utilisation de grenade pour faire reculer les manifestants. <a href="https://t.co/h6AvTDJP8K">pic.twitter.com/h6AvTDJP8K</a></p>
<p>— Clément Lanot (@ClementLanot) <a href="https://twitter.com/ClementLanot/status/1454452928274644999?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw">October 30, 2021</a></p></blockquote>
<p>“We know who Joseph Stalin was, we know the hundreds of thousands of deaths he caused, these people are only his distant descendants,” Zemmour said in reaction to the far-left extremists.</p>
<p>Footage of the violence, as well as graffiti <a href="https://twitter.com/ClementLanot/status/1454467175058706433?s=20">calling for Zemmour to be murdered</a>, was reported by French journalist Clément Lanot.</p>
<p class="a8d-pre">Prime Minister Janez Janša is not the first world leader to label Antifa as a terrorist group. Former U.S. President Donald J. Trump <a href="https://www.breitbart.com/politics/2021/01/06/trump-urges-mike-pompeo-to-classify-antifa-as-terrorist-group/">urged</a> former Secretary of State Mike Pompeo to designate Antifa as a terrorist group earlier this year before leaving office.</p>
<figure class="a8d adSo" id="M-ROS-B3"></figure>
<p>The year prior, former U.S. Attorney General William Barr <a href="https://www.breitbart.com/politics/2020/05/31/ag-barr-antifa-violence-is-domestic-terrorism-and-will-be-treat-accordingly/">stated</a> that Antifa members were involved in violence that amounted to domestic terrorism.</p>
<p>“The violence instigated and carried out by Antifa and other similar groups in connection with the rioting is domestic terrorism and will be treated accordingly,” Barr said.</p>
<p>In 2019, when President Trump first discussed declaring Antifa a terrorist group, left-wing figures across Europe, particularly in Germany, reacted by <a href="https://www.breitbart.com/europe/2019/07/30/weareantifa-trump-comments-triggers-outpouring-of-support-for-extreme-left-antifa-in-germany/">declaring their support</a> for the extremist group.</p>
