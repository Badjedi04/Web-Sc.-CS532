<h2><strong>Author’s Note and Update</strong></h2>
<p><i>The World has been in a state of crisis for more than two years despite the fact that the WHO and the CDC (with the usual innuendos) have unequivocally confirmed that </i><strong style="font-style: italic;">the RT-PCR test used to justify every single policy mandate including lockdowns, social distancing, the mask, confinement of the labor force, closure of economic activity, etc. is flawed and invalid.  </strong></p>
<p><em>This article was first published on March 21, 2021 focussing on the WHO’s Mea Culpa dated January 20, 2021. </em></p>
<p><em>The WHO advisory was then </em><em>followed a few months later by the bombshell decision of <a href="https://www.cdc.gov/csels/dls/locs/2021/07-21-2021-lab-alert-Changes_CDC_RT-PCR_SARS-CoV-2_Testing_1.html">the Centers for Disease Control and Prevention (CDC)  </a>(July 21, 2021) t<strong>o withdraw the</strong> <strong>PCR test as a valid method for detecting and identifying SARS-CoV-2.  </strong></em></p>
<p><em><strong>As of December, 31 2021, the PCR test is longer considered valid by the CDC in the U.S. </strong></em></p>
<p><i></i><i>For more details see</i></p>

<h2>The Mainstream Media Now Reluctantly Acknowledges that the PCR  Test is Flawed</h2>
<p><i></i>After having sustained the propaganda campaign, the mainstream media has now tacitly acknowledged that the <strong>PCR TEST IS INVALID</strong>.</p>
<p>Below is an excerpt from <a href="https://www.dailymail.co.uk/health/article-10606107/Did-flawed-tests-convince-Covid-worse-really-was.html">London’s Daily Mail</a> on something <strong>which has been known and documented by scientists and the independent media from the outset of the corona crisis in January 2020. </strong>The report is a convoluted. It is an obvious understatement:</p>
<p style="padding-left: 30px;">“Did flawed PCR tests convince us Covid was worse than it really was?  …</p>
<p style="padding-left: 30px;"><strong>It has been one of the most enduring Covid conspiracy theories</strong>: that the ‘<strong>gold standard’ PCR tests used to diagnose the virus</strong> were picking up people who weren’t actually infected.</p>
<p class="mol-para-with-font" style="padding-left: 30px;">Some even suggested the swabs, which have been carried out more than 200 million times in the UK alone, may mistake common colds and flu for corona.</p>
<p class="mol-para-with-font" style="padding-left: 30px;">If either, or both, were true, it would mean many of these cases should never have been counted in the daily tally – that the ominous and all-too-familiar figure, <strong>which was used to inform decisions on lockdowns and other pandemic measures</strong>, was an over-count. (<a href="https://www.dailymail.co.uk/health/article-10606107/Did-flawed-tests-convince-Covid-worse-really-was.html">Daily Mail,</a> March 12, 2022, emphasis added)</p>
<p>It is carefully worded with a view to protecting the decision-makers.</p>
<h2><strong>The PCR Test is the Smoking Gun. There is No Pandemic</strong></h2>
<p>We are not dealing with<strong> mistakes</strong> as suggested by the above media report.</p>
<p><strong>If the PCR test is invalid and meaningless, this means that there is no such thing as a “Covid-19 Confirmed Case”</strong></p>

<h2>Nucleic Acid Testing (NAT) Technologies that Use Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) for Detection of SARS-CoV-2</h2>
<p><em><strong>Product type:</strong> Nucleic acid testing (NAT) technologies that use polymerase chain reaction (PCR) for detection of SARS-CoV-2 </em></p>
<p><em><strong>Date:</strong> 13 January 2021                                                                       </em></p>
<p><em><strong>WHO-identifier:</strong> 2020/5, version 2 </em></p>
<p><em><strong>Target audience: </strong>laboratory professionals and users of IVDs.</em></p>
<p><strong>Purpose of this notice:</strong> clarify information previously provided by WHO. This notice supersedes WHO Information Notice for In Vitro Diagnostic Medical Device (IVD) Users 2020/05 version 1, issued 14 December 2020.</p>
<p><strong>Description of the problem: </strong>WHO requests users to follow the instructions for use (IFU) when interpreting results for specimens tested using PCR methodology.</p>
<p><strong>Users of IVDs must read and follo</strong>w the IFU carefully to determine if manual adjustment of the PCR positivity threshold is recommended by the manufacturer.</p>
<p>WHO guidance <a href="https://www.who.int/publications/i/item/diagnostic-testing-for-sars-cov-2" rel="noopener noreferrer" target="_blank">Diagnostic testing for SARS-CoV-2</a> states that careful interpretation of weak positive results is needed (<em>1</em>). The cycle threshold (Ct) needed to detect virus is inversely proportional to the patient’s viral load. Where test results do not correspond with the clinical presentation, a new specimen should be taken and retested using the same or different NAT technology.</p>
<p>WHO reminds IVD users that disease prevalence alters the predictive value of test results; as disease prevalence decreases, the risk of false positive increases (<em>2</em>). This means that the probability that a person who has a positive result (SARS-CoV-2 detected) is truly infected with SARS-CoV-2 decreases as prevalence decreases, irrespective of the claimed specificity.</p>
<p>Most PCR assays are indicated as an aid for diagnosis, therefore, health care providers must consider any result in combination with timing of sampling, specimen type, assay specifics, clinical observations, patient history, confirmed status of any contacts, and epidemiological information.</p>
<p><strong>Actions to be taken by IVD users:</strong></p>
<ol>
<li>Please read carefully the IFU in its entirety.</li>
<li>Contact your local representative if there is any aspect of the IFU that is unclear to you.</li>
<li>Check the IFU for each incoming consignment to detect any changes to the IFU.</li>
<li>Provide the Ct value in the report to the requesting health care provider.</li>
</ol>
<p><strong>Notes</strong></p>
<p>1. Diagnostic testing for SARS-CoV-2. Geneva: World Health Organization; 2020, WHO reference number WHO/2019-nCoV/laboratory/2020.6.</p>
<p>2. Altman DG, Bland JM. Diagnostic tests 2: Predictive values. BMJ. 1994 Jul 9;309(6947):102. doi: 10.1136/bmj.309.6947.102.</p>

<div class="bio"><h3>About the author:</h3>Michel Chossudovsky is an award-winning author, Professor of Economics (emeritus) at the University of Ottawa, Founder and Director of the Centre for Research on Globalization (CRG), Montreal, Editor of Global Research.  He has taught as visiting professor in Western Europe, Southeast Asia, the Pacific and Latin America. He has served as economic adviser to governments of developing countries and has acted as a consultant for several international organizations. He is the author of eleven books including The Globalization of Poverty and The New World Order (2003), America’s “War on Terrorism” (2005), The Global Economic Crisis, The Great Depression of the Twenty-first Century (2009) (Editor), Towards a World War III Scenario: The Dangers of Nuclear War (2011), The Globalization of War, America's Long War against Humanity (2015). He is a contributor to the Encyclopaedia Britannica.  His writings have been published in more than twenty languages. In 2014, he was awarded the Gold Medal for Merit of the Republic of Serbia for his writings on NATO's war of aggression against Yugoslavia.
