The WFP also attracted 150,000 votes on its line for Sen. <a data-saferedirecturl="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://keywiki.org/Charles_Schumer&amp;source=gmail&amp;ust=1589997499786000&amp;usg=AFQjCNEEqKpp89FwhW6FribHzL-3hEmRXQ" href="https://keywiki.org/Charles_Schumer">Charles Schumer</a>, and about 120,000 for Kerry. <a data-saferedirecturl="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://keywiki.org/War_Resisters_League&amp;source=gmail&amp;ust=1589997499786000&amp;usg=AFQjCNEzOqBTHluGryOEn5v1eG8RVouTCA" href="https://keywiki.org/War_Resisters_League">War Resisters League</a> leader <a data-saferedirecturl="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://keywiki.org/David_McReynolds&amp;source=gmail&amp;ust=1589997499786000&amp;usg=AFQjCNGj2dMFAB48zg9CkcZ5ttZDA4EZEg" href="https://keywiki.org/David_McReynolds">David McReynolds</a> ran against Schumer as a Green Party candidate and garnered 33,000 votes.</em></strong></p>
<p style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>In 2004, the <a data-saferedirecturl="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://keywiki.org/Working_Families_Party&amp;source=gmail&amp;ust=1589997499786000&amp;usg=AFQjCNGgroe21SweZiT51kKI_7loo6Ky2Q" href="https://keywiki.org/Working_Families_Party">Working Families Party</a> endorsed Democrat Charles Schumer for U.S. Senate. Thus, Schumer’s name appeared on the ballot twice — once as a Democrat and once as the candidate of the Working Families Party. Supporters of the Working Families Party — 155,000 of them — voted for Schumer on the Working Families Party ballot line.<sup><a data-saferedirecturl="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://keywiki.org/Charles_E._Schumer%23cite_note-14&amp;source=gmail&amp;ust=1589997499786000&amp;usg=AFQjCNHxVmOXCKcr6tUnl3LeXalEHMbhBg" href="https://keywiki.org/Charles_E._Schumer#cite_note-14">[14]</a></sup></strong></p>
<h3>WFP pre-election gathering</h3>
<p style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>Many high-power politicians–United States Senator <a data-saferedirecturl="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://keywiki.org/Charles_Schumer&amp;source=gmail&amp;ust=1589997499786000&amp;usg=AFQjCNEEqKpp89FwhW6FribHzL-3hEmRXQ" href="https://keywiki.org/Charles_Schumer">Charles Schumer</a>, Mayor <a data-saferedirecturl="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://keywiki.org/Bill_de_Blasio&amp;source=gmail&amp;ust=1589997499786000&amp;usg=AFQjCNFOmpmq5go-3NdY5fEg0aC3q6i7zQ" href="https://keywiki.org/Bill_de_Blasio">Bill de Blasio</a>, Council Speaker <a data-saferedirecturl="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://keywiki.org/Melissa_Mark-Viverito&amp;source=gmail&amp;ust=1589997499786000&amp;usg=AFQjCNGj-HWv99kRNaTdmyK9X186GZb6Xg" href="https://keywiki.org/Melissa_Mark-Viverito">Melissa Mark-Viverito</a>, Public Advocate <a data-saferedirecturl="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://keywiki.org/Letitia_James&amp;source=gmail&amp;ust=1589997499786000&amp;usg=AFQjCNGScGI39opul697ERN5qJs8pVvZqA" href="https://keywiki.org/Letitia_James">Letitia James</a> and State Senator <a data-saferedirecturl="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://keywiki.org/James_Sanders&amp;source=gmail&amp;ust=1589997499786000&amp;usg=AFQjCNFhksExm0N60Ye-reltyFuhV_ScQA" href="https://keywiki.org/James_Sanders">James Sanders</a>–fired up a hundreds-strong crowd of supporters of the labor-backed <a data-saferedirecturl="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://keywiki.org/Working_Families_Party&amp;source=gmail&amp;ust=1589997499786000&amp;usg=AFQjCNGgroe21SweZiT51kKI_7loo6Ky2Q" href="https://keywiki.org/Working_Families_Party">Working Families Party</a> 9/9/2014 with speeches vowing to keep Republicans from controlling the State Senate, to raise the minimum wage, to create a public financing system for state elections and to organize low-paid fast food and airport workers.</strong></p>
<p style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>The crowd, a mix of union members and activist groups like <a data-saferedirecturl="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://keywiki.org/index.php?title%3DVocal-NY%26action%3Dedit%26redlink%3D1&amp;source=gmail&amp;ust=1589997499786000&amp;usg=AFQjCNF62rYYfLkKqHdjzknjob0q0osB8g" href="https://keywiki.org/index.php?title=Vocal-NY&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1">Vocal-NY</a> and <a data-saferedirecturl="https://www.google.com/url?q=https://keywiki.org/New_York_Communities_for_Change&amp;source=gmail&amp;ust=1589997499787000&amp;usg=AFQjCNEiOcQVulYv89oVACJyURmB3AiqeA" href="https://keywiki.org/New_York_Communities_for_Change">New York Communities for Change</a>, crammed into the basement of Manhattan’s Saint Vartan’s Armenian Cathedral for the event, entitled “Justice for Workers.” The evening started with a reading by poet Maria “Mariposa” Fernandez, who read a piece detailing the difficult life of a Hispanic service worker that simultaneously railed against pollution, an America “ruled by warmongering men,” “U.S. colonialism and imperialism” and the Atlantic slave trade.</strong></p>
<p style="font-weight: 400;"><strong>“We’re not going to take back one Senate, we’re going to take back two Senates, one in Washington and one in Albany!” said Mr. Schumer, recalling his long relationship with WFP, which dates back to his first successful run for his seat in 1998–when Republicans dominated both houses of the federal legislature. “We sent Al D’Amato back to Island Park, and we put Newt Gingrich on the midnight train to Georgia. Now we’re going to do that again!”</strong></p>
