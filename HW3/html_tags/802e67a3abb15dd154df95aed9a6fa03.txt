<h1>Jill Biden Mocked over Attempt at Speaking Spanish with Flag Choice Questioned as Backdrop</h1>

<a class="button_facebook" href="#" rel="nofollow" role="button" title="Facebook"><em></em><span class="acz5">15,218</span></a>

<div class="entry-content">
<p class="subheading">First Lady Jill Biden journeyed to California on Wednesday in a visit to a pop-up vaccination site for farmworkers designed to encourage others to join the national mass coronavirus vaccination program.</p><div class="H" id="gmx-rev-top"></div>
<p class="a8d-pre">Video of her effort to speak Spanish to around 100 farm workers during the stop in Delano attracted almost as much attention as the flag that provided the backdrop. It came on the birthday of César Chávez, a left-wing labor leader, and she was joined by California Gov. Gavin Newsom.</p>
<figure class="a8d" id="D-ROS-B1"></figure>
<figure class="a8d" id="M-ROS-B1"></figure><figure class="H" id="gmxrevmore"></figure>
<p>“It’s ‘Si se puede’ (Yes you/we can) not ‘Si se pwadueh,’” RGA Deputy Communications Director Joanna Rodriguez <a href="https://twitter.com/joannamrod/status/1377420575497412621">tweeted</a>. “I can’t even imagine what word she was trying to say. Seriously why even try pandering if you’re going to butcher it and not practice before?!”</p>
<p>Others were quick to join the commentary:</p>
<blockquote class="a8d-pre twitter-tweet" data-dnt="true" data-width="550">
<p dir="ltr" lang="en">Me to my high school Spanish cohorts when we're sitting down to the final without studying at all. <a href="https://t.co/LENk5LRzBQ">pic.twitter.com/LENk5LRzBQ</a></p>
<p>— Matt Whitlock (@mattdizwhitlock) <a href="https://twitter.com/mattdizwhitlock/status/1377414374072066051?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw">April 1, 2021</a></p></blockquote>
<figure class="a8d" id="D-ROS-B2"></figure>
<figure class="a8d" id="M-ROS-B2"></figure>
<blockquote class="twitter-tweet" data-dnt="true" data-width="550">
<p dir="ltr" lang="en">Watching Jill Biden botch basic Spanish makes me miss Melania Trump, an extraordinary First Lady who is fluent in five languages.</p>
<p>— Nick Adams (@NickAdamsinUSA) <a href="https://twitter.com/NickAdamsinUSA/status/1377545818819997697?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw">April 1, 2021</a></p></blockquote>
<p>“Si se what???? I’m sure some one will surely tell Jill Biden ‘puadray’ is NOT a word in the Spanish dictionary,” Journalist Dania Alexandrino, a native Spanish speaker, <a href="https://twitter.com/DaniaPeriodista/status/1377437377963040768">tweeted</a>. “For those who are celebrating her effort, yeah NO! Very far from ‘Si se Puede’ which means ‘Yes (we) can.’ We in parenthesis because it can also be yes you can!”</p>
<p>The <a href="https://www.fresnobee.com/news/local/article250325196.html">Fresno Bee</a> also pointed to what it called the “gaffe.”</p>
<p>The Daily Wire <a href="https://www.dailywire.com/news/jill-biden-tries-to-speak-spanish-fails-badly-journalists-call-out-flag-behind-her">reports</a> the clip soon went viral prompting journalists and commentators to <a href="https://twitter.com/stillgray/status/1377402979871977473?ref_src=twsrc%5Etfw%7Ctwcamp%5Etweetembed%7Ctwterm%5E1377402979871977473%7Ctwgr%5E%7Ctwcon%5Es1_&amp;ref_url=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.ibtimes.sg%2Fdid-jill-biden-deliver-speech-front-nazi-flag-cesar-chavez-day-56552">question</a> the red and black flag used as a backdrop.</p>
<p class="a8d-pre">Critics claimed it appeared to resemble Nazi imagery and the Reichsadler (Eagle of the German Empire), national insignia of Nazi Germany, flown from 1935 until 1945.</p>
<figure class="a8d adSo" id="M-ROS-B3"></figure>
<p>The flag was in fact nothing of the kind and the allegation wrong.</p>
<p>Rather it is the official flag of the United Farm Workers of America. The organization <a href="https://ufw.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/01/UFWTrademarkfaq-5-18-16.pdf">asserts</a> the eagle featured on the standard was designed by César Chávez’s brother, Richard Chávez.</p>
<div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_16157984" style="max-width: 758px"><a href="https://media.breitbart.com/media/2021/04/GettyImages-1232047634.jpg"><img alt="U.S. First Lady Jill Biden speaks during her visit to The Forty Acres, the first headquarters of the United Farm Workers labor union, in Delano, California on March 31, 2021. (MANDEL NGAN/POOL/AFP via Getty Images)" class="wp-image-16157984 size-large" height="1024" src="https://media.breitbart.com/media/2021/04/GettyImages-1232047634-748x1024.jpg" width="748"/></a><p class="wp-caption-text"><em>U.S. First Lady Jill Biden speaks during her visit to The Forty Acres, the first headquarters of the United Farm Workers labor union, in Delano, California on March 31, 2021. (MANDEL NGAN/POOL/AFP via Getty Images)</em></p></div>
<p>César Chávez co-founded the United Farm Workers of America (UFW) union and was a staunch opponent of illegal immigration, as Breitbart News has previously <a href="https://www.breitbart.com/entertainment/2014/03/25/zuckerberg-ufw-using-cesar-chavez-movie-to-push-for-amnesty-legislation-late-labor-leader-abhorred/">reported</a>.</p>
<p>Even so, just hours before Jill Biden spoke President Joe Biden was also doing his best to contribute to the political makeover of the anti-migration, pro-American, labor-rights hero.</p>
<p>As Breitbart News <a href="https://www.breitbart.com/economy/2021/03/31/joe-biden-portrays-cesar-chavez-as-cheap-labor-amnesty-advocate/">reported</a>, the president pushed the left-wing makeover in a <a class="" href="https://www.whitehouse.gov/briefing-room/presidential-actions/2021/03/31/a-proclamation-on-cesar-chavez-day-2021/" rel="noopener external" target="_blank">March 31 statement</a>, saying: “I was proud to place a bust of César Chávez in the Oval Office, so that no one who enters that historic room may forget the powerful truths his farm worker hands imparted.”</p>
