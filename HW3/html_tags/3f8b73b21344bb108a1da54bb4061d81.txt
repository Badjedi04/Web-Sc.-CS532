<div class="clearfix text-formatted field field--name-body" property="schema:text"><p><iframe allow="autoplay" frameborder="no" height="166" scrolling="no" src="https://w.soundcloud.com/player/?url=https%3A//api.soundcloud.com/tracks/1017313447&amp;color=%23ff5500&amp;auto_play=false&amp;hide_related=false&amp;show_comments=true&amp;show_user=true&amp;show_reposts=false&amp;show_teaser=true" width="100%"></iframe></p>
<div style="font-size: 10px; color: #cccccc;line-break: anywhere;word-break: normal;overflow: hidden;white-space: nowrap;text-overflow: ellipsis; font-family: Interstate,Lucida Grande,Lucida Sans Unicode,Lucida Sans,Garuda,Verdana,Tahoma,sans-serif;font-weight: 100;"><a href="https://soundcloud.com/ukcolumn" style="color: #cccccc; text-decoration: none;" target="_blank" title="UK Column Live">UK Column Live</a> · <a href="https://soundcloud.com/ukcolumn/a-dissidents-guide-to-the-constitution-episode-5-part-i-democracy-a-british-value" style="color: #cccccc; text-decoration: none;" target="_blank" title="A Dissident's Guide to the Constitution: Episode 5, Part I — Democracy: a British value?">A Dissident's Guide to the Constitution: Episode 5, Part I — Democracy: a British value?</a></div>
<p>The claim is often made that Parliament is sovereign. Often, "Parliament" is taken to mean "not the Crown", or that the Crown should refrain from exercising a political role even if it is <a href="https://www.cambridge.org/core/books/veiled-sceptre/E6C6B2D0F14EA359BDD52F7F9C0D1D73">grudgingly acknowledged</a> to have one.</p>
<p>Is it right that the Queen has <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GPTsa6yyiHM">bowed out of politics</a>? What was she taught at an <a href="https://books.google.com/books?id=pKRjAwAAQBAJ&amp;pg=PA171">impressionable age</a> (from twelve onwards) about her constitutional role? Why do UK Column News presenters voice consistent scepticism about the proposal of "direct" or "participatory" democracy and the likelihood that those chosen to have their say will be briefed by the <a href="https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=yDH0KGQD9vw">Klaus Schwab</a>s of this world? Isn't democracy a <a href="https://www.youngcitizens.org/british-values">Fundamental British Value</a> these days? What did household names C.S. Lewis and Lord Hailsham have to say in the post-war generation about democracy?</p>
<p>Join David Scott, Mike Robinson and Alex Thomson for the first half of Episode 5 of <em><a href="https://www.ukcolumn.org/constitution/">A Dissident's Guide to the Constitution</a></em>, entitled <em>Democracy - a British Value?</em> The second half of the episode, released separately, will build on this ground to cover perhaps the <a href="https://books.google.com/books?id=fDM6CgAAQBAJ&amp;printsec=frontcover">best treatment so far written</a> of what is wrong and unhistorical about the notion that a house of elected legislators, controlled by <a href="https://www.ukcolumn.org/community/wp-content/uploads/2018/12/HOW-POLITICAL-PARTIES-CORRUPT-December-version-5-1.pdf">parties</a>, is the cure for all ills.</p>
