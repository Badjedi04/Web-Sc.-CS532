<div class="wp-caption alignnone" id="attachment_29666" style="width: 730px"><a href="https://cdn1.opensecrets.org/news/wp-content/uploads/2019/02/01160357/AIPAC-Israel-conference.jpg"><img alt="AIPAC Israel conference" aria-describedby="caption-attachment-29666" class="size-full wp-image-29666" height="426" loading="lazy" sizes="(max-width: 720px) 100vw, 720px" src="https://cdn1.opensecrets.org/news/wp-content/uploads/2019/02/01160357/AIPAC-Israel-conference.jpg" srcset="https://cdn1.opensecrets.org/news/wp-content/uploads/2019/02/01160357/AIPAC-Israel-conference.jpg 720w, https://cdn1.opensecrets.org/news/wp-content/uploads/2019/02/01160357/AIPAC-Israel-conference-300x178.jpg 300w" width="720"/></a><p class="wp-caption-text" id="caption-attachment-29666">David Friedman, United States Ambassador to Israel, speaking at the AIPAC Policy Conference (Michael Brochstein/SOPA Images/LightRocket via Getty Images)</p></div>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Freshmen</span><a href="https://www.opensecrets.org/members-of-congress/summary?cid=N00043581&amp;newmem=Y"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Rep. Ilhan Omar</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> (D-Minn.) garnered considerable </span><a href="https://www.nbcnews.com/politics/congress/freshman-democratic-rep-ilhan-omar-accused-sending-anti-semitic-tweets-n969946"><span style="font-weight: 400;">criticism</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> from Democrats and Republicans for comments she made regarding the </span><a href="https://www.opensecrets.org/lobby/clientsum.php?id=D000046963&amp;year=2018"><span style="font-weight: 400;">American-Israel Public Affairs Committee</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> (AIPAC) deemed by many as anti-Semitic. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Omar </span><a href="https://twitter.com/IlhanMN/status/1094761790595088384"><span style="font-weight: 400;">tweeted</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> that AIPAC, a group which doesnt actually have its own PAC, was paying Congress members, specifically Minority Leader Kevin McCarthy (R-Calif.), to support Israel. House Speaker Nancy Pelosi (D-Calif.) in a joint statement with Democratic leaders </span><a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/nation/2019/02/11/its-all-about-benjamins-baby-ilhan-omar-again-accused-anti-semitism-over-tweets/?utm_term=.4d1c203e9236"><span style="font-weight: 400;">condemned</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> Omars remarks on Monday. Omar apologized in </span><a href="https://twitter.com/IlhanMN/status/1095046561254567937"><span style="font-weight: 400;">a tweet</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> on Monday afternoon, but said that I reaffirm the problematic role of lobbyists in our politics, whether it be AIPAC, the NRA or the fossil fuel industry.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Though AIPAC as an organization cannot contribute to campaigns, individuals who work for the organization have. In the 2018 election cycle, individuals affiliated with the organization </span><a href="https://www.opensecrets.org/orgs/toprecips.php?id=D000046963&amp;cycle=2018"><span style="font-weight: 400;">donated</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> just $21,350 to 14 different candidates, nine of whom were Democrats. No one associated with AIPAC gave any contributions to McCarthy.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">AIPAC said in a </span><a href="https://twitter.com/AIPAC/status/1094788295199023104"><span style="font-weight: 400;">tweet</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> on Sunday night that We are proud that we are engaged in the democratic process to strengthen the US-Israel relationship. Our bipartisan efforts are reflective of American values and interests. We will not be deterred in any way by ill-informed and illegitimate attacks on this important work.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">AIPAC is a significant player in terms of lobbying, accounting for the vast majority of lobbying spending by pro-Israel groups, spending more than $3.5 million in 2018. The organization was </span><a href="https://www.washingtonpost.com/news/powerpost/wp/2015/09/03/how-aipac-lost-the-iran-deal-fight/?noredirect=on&amp;utm_term=.2029c7ef2cd9"><span style="font-weight: 400;">staunchly opposed</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> to the Iran Nuclear Deal in 2015, lobbied extensively against it and </span><a href="https://www.aipac.org/learn/resources/aipac-publications/publication?pubpath=PolicyPolitics/Press/AIPAC%20Statements/2018/05/AIPAC%20Statement%20on%20Iran%20Nuclear%20Deal%20Announcement"><span style="font-weight: 400;">supported</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> the Trump administrations withdrawal from the agreement. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Other pro-Israel groups are also prominent and active political spending forces each cycle. The 2018 midterms saw groups in the movement </span><a href="https://www.opensecrets.org/industries/totals.php?cycle=2018&amp;ind=Q05"><span style="font-weight: 400;">contribute</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> more than $14.8 million, the highest total for them in a midterm since 1990. Contributions went predominantly towards Democrats who received 63 percent. </span><a href="https://www.opensecrets.org/members-of-congress/summary?cid=N00000699"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Sen. Robert Menendez</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> (D-N.J.) received by far the </span><a href="https://www.opensecrets.org/industries/recips.php?cycle=2018&amp;ind=Q05"><span style="font-weight: 400;">most money</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> from pro-Israel groups in 2018 with $546,507. In comparison, McCarthy </span><a href="https://www.opensecrets.org/industries/summary.php?ind=Q05&amp;cycle=2018&amp;recipdetail=H&amp;mem=Y"><span style="font-weight: 400;">received</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> just $33,200 from pro-Israel groups in the midterms.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">In all, 269 members of the House and 57 members of the Senate </span><a href="https://www.opensecrets.org/industries/summary.php?cycle=2018&amp;ind=Q05"><span style="font-weight: 400;">received</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> some monetary contributions from pro-Israel interests in 2018.</span></p>

<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The most involved group in terms of political contributions in the 2018 cycle was </span><a href="https://www.opensecrets.org/orgs/totals.php?id=D000052457&amp;cycle=2018"><span style="font-weight: 400;">JStreet</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, an organization </span><a href="https://donate.jstreetpac.org/about-us/"><span style="font-weight: 400;">which supports</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> strong U.S.-Israel relations and a two-state solution to the Israel-Palestine conflict. Contributions </span><a href="https://www.opensecrets.org/orgs/toprecips.php?id=D000052457&amp;cycle=2018"><span style="font-weight: 400;">totaled more</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> than $4 million, the majority of contributions came from individuals, all of which went to Democratic candidates. Their PAC, </span><a href="https://www.opensecrets.org/pacs/pacgot.php?cycle=2018&amp;cmte=C00441949"><span style="font-weight: 400;">JStreetPAC</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, contributed $416,437 to federal candidates in 2018. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The top overall JStreet recipient was </span><a href="https://www.opensecrets.org/races/summary?id=WI06&amp;cycle=2018"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Dan Kohl</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> (D-Wis.) with $228,983. Kohl, who is on the </span><a href="https://jstreet.org/about-us/board-of-directors/dan-kohl/#.XGGkM1VKi70"><span style="font-weight: 400;">board of directors</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> for JStreet, lost his race for a House seat in Wisconsin. </span><a href="https://www.opensecrets.org/members-of-congress/summary/tammy-baldwin?cid=N00004367"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Sen. Tammy Baldwin</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> (D-Wis.) received the second-most with $206,705.</span></p>
<p><a href="https://www.opensecrets.org/orgs/summary.php?id=D000022158"><span style="font-weight: 400;">NorPAC</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;">, a </span><a href="http://norpac.net/about"><span style="font-weight: 400;">nonpartisan PAC</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> with the goal of supporting members and candidates who demonstrate a genuine commitment to the strength, security, and survival of Israel, spent more than $1.1 million in the 2018 cycle, with much of it going to Democrats. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">The group which spent the most on Republican candidates was the </span><a href="https://www.opensecrets.org/orgs/summary.php?id=D000028612"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Republican Jewish Coalition</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> which contributed $501,097 during the midterms. The groups biggest contribution, $42,474, went to the campaign of </span><a href="https://www.opensecrets.org/members-of-congress/summary?cid=N00033085&amp;cycle=2018"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Sen. Ted Cruz</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> (R-Texas). On the Republican Jewish Coalition </span><a href="http://www.rjchq.org/leadership"><span style="font-weight: 400;">board</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> is the largest individual donor from the 2018 cycle, Sheldon Adelson. Also holding a board seat is another big money donor, co-founder of Home Depot, Bernie Marcus. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">Pro-Israel groups also </span><a href="https://www.opensecrets.org/industries/lobbying.php?cycle=2018&amp;ind=Q05"><span style="font-weight: 400;">spent</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> a significant amount of money on lobbying in 2018 with more than $5 million, the highest ever since tracking began in 1998. </span></p>
<p><span style="font-weight: 400;">A variety of other groups other than AIPAC spend some money on lobbying, such as the </span><a href="https://www.opensecrets.org/lobby/clientsum.php?id=D000071422&amp;cycle=2018"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Israeli-American Coalition for Action</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> with $550,000 and JStreet with $300,000. Bills like the </span><a href="https://www.congress.gov/bill/115th-congress/senate-bill/720"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Israel Anti-Boycott Act</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> and </span><a href="https://www.congress.gov/bill/115th-congress/senate-bill/170"><span style="font-weight: 400;">Combating BDS Act of 2017</span></a><span style="font-weight: 400;"> received plenty of the lobbying attention.</span></p>
